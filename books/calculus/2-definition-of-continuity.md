We say a function $f(x)$ is *continuous* at $x=a$ if $f$ satisfies

$$\lim_{x\to a^+}f(x)=\lim_{x\to a^-}=f(a).$$

In terms of $\varepsilon-\delta$, for any $\varepsilon>0$, there exists a $\delta>0$ such that we have

$$|f(x)-f(a)|<\varepsilon$$

whenever $|x-a|<\delta$.
